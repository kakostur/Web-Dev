Little Women

Written by Louisa May Alcott

About the Book

Little Women is a classic novel published in 1868. It tells the story of the March sisters—Meg, Jo, Beth, and Amy—as they grow up during the American Civil War.

Memorable Quote

"I am not afraid of storms, for I am learning how to sail my ship." - Louisa May Alcott

Characters

Character Role
Meg March The eldest sister, responsible and kind.
Jo March The tomboyish, aspiring writer.
Beth March The shy, musical sister.
Amy March The youngest, artistic sister.

Key Themes

  • Family and Sisterhood
  • Love and Sacrifice
  • Independence and Personal Growth

Louisa May Alcott

Author's Background

Louisa May Alcott (1832–1888) was an American novelist and poet. She grew up in a family of transcendentalists and supported women's rights and abolitionism.
